Step 1

Reading the Document

You can submit a privacy policy or EULA two ways:

Paste a URL. We fetch the page directly from the company's website and extract the full text of the policy. We read the actual source — not a cached or summarized version.

Upload a PDF. If a policy is behind a login or only available as a document, you can save it as a PDF from your browser and upload it. We extract all text from the PDF before analysis begins.

Once we have the text, the entire document is passed to an AI model (Anthropic's Claude) which reads it the way a privacy attorney would — looking for what the company can do, not just what they say they won't do.

How the AI is instructed: The AI is told to assume the worst-case interpretation of any vague or ambiguous language, because companies write policies to maximize their own options, not to protect you. If a category is not addressed in the policy at all, it is automatically rated High risk — silence favors the company, never you.

Step 3

The Data Collection Deep Dive — 39 Questions

Data Collection receives special treatment because it is the foundation of every other privacy risk. A company cannot sell, share, or misuse data it has not collected. But data collected only for your benefit and kept entirely within the company is far less dangerous than the same data handed to third parties.

To capture this distinction, every analysis includes a second, separate AI examination: 39 specific questions across 9 data types, each answered directly from the policy text. This replaces a single broad AI judgment with a structured, point-scored evaluation.

Step 4

The Third-Party Sharing Multiplier

This is the most important design decision in our scoring model.

Data held by the company that collected it is governed by the terms and conditions you agreed to. You know who has it. You can read their policy. You may have some legal recourse.

Data shared with third parties is a fundamentally different threat. Once your data leaves the collecting company:

  • You never see the contracts between that company and its data partners
  • Those third parties have their own sharing arrangements you are not party to
  • Your data can propagate through an unlimited chain of companies you have never heard of
  • You have no visibility, no recourse, and no ability to trace where it ends up

For this reason, third-party sharing is weighted four times heavier than collection alone in our Data Collection scoring formula:

# Per-question score (max 5.0 when both are "Yes")
score = (collection_risk × 1)
      + (collection_risk × sharing_risk × 4)

The 12 weighted scores are summed, normalized to a 0–100 scale (where 0 is the safest possible policy and 100 is the most harmful), and converted to a letter grade.

Transparency & Limitations

All analysis and ratings on this site are opinions generated by artificial intelligence and are intended for informational purposes only. They do not constitute legal advice and should not be relied upon as statements of fact. Always consult a qualified attorney for legal guidance.
  • We analyze what the policy says, not what the company does. A company can write a restrictive policy and still violate it — or write a permissive policy and behave responsibly. We score the document, not the company's actual behavior.
  • Policies change. We record the date of each analysis and flag when a policy has been updated since it was last analyzed. Always check the document date against the company's current published policy.
  • The AI can be wrong. We instruct the AI to be conservative and assume the worst-case reading of ambiguous language. In rare cases it may misread a clause. Every category analysis includes a verbatim quote from the document so you can verify the original language yourself.
  • Silence is scored as risk. If a category is not addressed in the policy, we rate it High. This is intentional — a company that doesn't address your right to delete your data has not protected it.
  • We do not track you. We do not store anything about your session, your IP address, or your identity. The only data we retain is the analysis of the policies themselves.
